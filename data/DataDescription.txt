1. The Measurement Type (AB vs. COH)
The very first letters tell you what is being measured:
AB (Absolute Power): This measures the raw strength or amplitude of the brainwave at a specific spot. Think of it as how "loud" that part of the brain is talking.
COH (Coherence): This measures how well two different parts of the brain are synchronized. Think of it as measuring whether two parts of the brain are communicating with each other. This is why the COH columns list two locations in their name.
2. The Frequency Band (delta, gamma, etc.)
Brainwaves happen at different speeds (frequencies), and different speeds correspond to different mental states. The words like delta or gamma (and the letters like A or F next to them) tell you which frequency is being measured:
Delta: The slowest brainwaves, usually seen in deep sleep or unconsciousness.
Theta: Slow waves related to daydreaming, sleep, or deep relaxation.
Alpha: Relaxed, calm, awake states (like when your eyes are closed but you are awake).
Beta: Normal waking consciousness, active thinking, and focus.
Gamma: The fastest waves, associated with high-level concentration, problem-solving, and peak mental activity.
3. The Electrode Location (FP1, T3, Pz, etc.)
The letters and numbers at the end of the column name refer to the exact location of the sensor on the scalp using the international "10-20 system." The lowercase letters (like .a., .g.) are just alphabetical counters to keep the list organized.
Here is what the capital letters mean:
F (Frontal): Front of the head (decision making, problem-solving). FP stands for Frontal Pole (very front).
T (Temporal): Side of the head (memory, emotion, hearing).
C (Central): Top middle of the head (motor control).
P (Parietal): Upper back of the head (sensory processing, spatial awareness).
O (Occipital): Very back of the head (visual processing).
Note: Odd numbers (like F3, T5) are on the left side of the brain. Even numbers (like F4, T6) are on the right side. A "z" (like Pz, Cz) means it is dead center in the middle of the head.
Let's Translate a Few Examples:
Example 1: AB.A.delta.a.FP1
AB: Absolute power (strength).
delta: Delta brainwave frequency (slow waves).
FP1: Left Frontal Pole (front-left part of the brain).
Translation: The strength of the slow delta brainwaves measured at the front-left of the patient's head.
Example 2: COH.A.delta.g.F8.h.T3
COH: Coherence (communication/sync).
delta: Delta brainwave frequency.
F8: Right Frontal lobe.
T3: Left Temporal lobe.
Translation: How synchronized the slow delta waves are between the right front of the brain and the left side of the brain.
Because researchers measure 5 different frequency bands, across 19 different scalp locations, and check the coherence between every single combination of those locations, the number of columns quickly multiplies into the thousands!
The Full Breakdown: COH.F.gamma.l.T4.o.Pz
Part 1: COH = Coherence (The measurement type).
Part 2: F = Frequency Group Code. This is simply a redundant letter used by the software that exported the data to group the frequencies together. A is delta, B is theta, C is alpha, D is beta, E is highbeta, and F is gamma.
Part 3: gamma = The actual frequency band.
Part 4: l = Channel Sorting Prefix. This is just an alphabetical counter attached to the electrode T4.
Part 5: T4 = The first electrode location (Right Temporal).
Part 6: o = Channel Sorting Prefix. Another alphabetical counter attached to Pz.
Part 7: Pz = The second electrode location (Parietal Midline).
