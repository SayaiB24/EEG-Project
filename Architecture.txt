### **Detailed Component Breakdown**

**1. Data Acquisition (The Smart Endpoints)**

* 
**Source:** Students engaged in standardized classroom tasks or simulated lectures.


* 
**Sensors:** Wireless EEG headsets capture raw neural electrical activity. Simultaneously, wearables capture Heart Rate Variability (HRV) , and digital platforms collect standardized psychological questionnaire responses (PHQ-9, GAD-7).


* 
**Transmission:** This high-frequency data is transmitted continuously via Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE) to the local campus server.



**2. Preprocessing Layer (Signal Cleaning)**

* 
**Input:** High-frequency, noisy raw EEG data.


* 
**Filtering:** Band-pass filters are applied to isolate the specific frequencies of interest (typically 0.5 to 50 Hz) and remove environmental electrical hum.


* 
**Artifact Removal:** Algorithms like Independent Component Analysis (ICA) and FASTER automatically identify and strip out non-brain noise, such as eye blinks and muscle tension.


* **Output:** Purified, 1D cortical time-series signals.

**3. Feature Extraction Layer (Translating the Brainwaves)**

* 
**Transformation:** Short-Time Fourier Transform (STFT) and Welch's method convert the 1D time-series data into dynamic 2D spectrograms.


* 
**Quantification:** The system calculates Power Spectral Density (PSD) to measure the absolute strength of critical frequency bands, such as Theta, Alpha, and Beta.


* 
**Biomarker Generation:** Key clinical ratios are computed from this spectral data, notably the Theta/Beta Ratio (TBR) for tracking attention and Alpha Asymmetry for detecting depressive states.


* **Output:** Structured datasets consisting of 2D Spectrogram matrices and discrete numerical biomarker vectors.

**4. Main Model & Multi-Modal Fusion Layer (The Inference Engine)**

* 
**Integration:** The system fuses the structural EEG data with the physiological HRV metrics and psychological survey scores into a single, unified profile.


* 
**Spatial Analysis:** 2D Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) process the spectrograms as images to identify spatial clinical patterns.


* 
**Temporal Analysis:** Bidirectional LSTMs (Bi-LSTM) analyze the sequence of the data to track how the student's brain states shift over the duration of the lecture.


* 
**Explainability:** Explainable AI algorithms (SHAP and LIME) analyze the model's decision pathways to highlight exactly which physiological marker triggered a specific alert.


* 
**Output:** Real-time, highly interpretable "mental health risk scores" and continuous attention indices.



**5. Deployment Layer (Action & Storage)**

* 
**Edge Processing (Immediate):** The entire AI inference pipeline runs locally on an Edge Hub (e.g., a campus mini-PC) to provide near-instantaneous feedback and automated micro-break triggers directly in the classroom.


* 
**Cloud Core (Long-Term):** De-identified, encrypted metadata is securely pushed to a central cloud repository.


* 
**Output:** Immediate triage notifications for on-campus counselors and secure, longitudinal population dashboards for educational administrators.
